Edge Detection

Edge detection can be done in different ways, all with linear algebra at their core. As you know, images can be represented with matrices. These matrices can then be manipulated to do cool things such as edge detection. First, we will introduce a new matrix operation called kernel convolution. Never heard of that? Think it sounds like corn? Are you getting hungry for popcorn? Well, there's no popcorn, but it does involve taking a matrix called a kernel and traversing another matrix, taking same-sized kernels out of that. The kernels of that matrix are centered around the specific element that you're on when traversing the matrix. The elements of the two kernels are multiplied together. The elements of the resulting kernel are then averaged. This average is then used to replace the specific element of the matrix (the one that isn't the kernel). By replace, we actually mean that you should put it in a separate same-sized matrix so you can preserve the values of the original matrix.

Here, we will walk through Sobel edge detection on a simple picture of a flower.

After both convolutions, we took the magnitude of the values from the x and y directions.

Here is the formula for the magnitude:

G = √(Gx2 + Gy2)

There is also a method called the Canny edge detector. This allows us to obtain cleaner edges. This doesn't do much on simple images (like the flower image in our example).

Gaussian Blur:


Step one is to perform a Gaussian blur on the image. To do this, you must perform kernel convolution with this matrix as the kernel:

1 2 1
2 4 2
1 2 1

After the Sobel process:

Step two is to perform the Sobel process (as detailed above) on the blurred image.

Step three involves edge thinning. Find the orientation of the edge at a particular element of the image matrix with this expression:

arctan(Gx / Gy)

Using this, the element is then compared to its neighboring elements. This is done across the edge and not along it.

Hysteresis thresholding is used to find only dominant edges and filter out the weaker ones.
